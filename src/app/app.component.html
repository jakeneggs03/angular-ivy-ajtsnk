Introduction

Mike decided to start Net Neutrality Brewing back in 2015. He started with simple brewing kits but quickly developed into creating his own reciptes. Creating his own recipes consists of buying specific types of grain, hops, and spices.

About

The name, Net Neutrality Brewing, is exactly what it sounds like: from the Open Internet Order in 2015. Coincidentally, this order was passed exactly the same day that Ohleger brewed his first beer. Because of this, Michael named his home brewery Net Neutrality Brewing or NNB for short. This is also the reason that each of his beers are named something computer related such as TCP/IP(A), Y2K Imperial Red Ale, or Cold War, Dark Web Imperial Russian Stout. Even looking at the labels they are spelled out in ASCII, hex, or Binary.
